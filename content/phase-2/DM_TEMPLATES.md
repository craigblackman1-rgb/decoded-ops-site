## Timing & Cadence Guidance

**When to send:**
- **Monday–Friday, 9–11 AM UK time:** highest open rate, business mindset
- **Avoid weekends:** doesn't feel professional
- **Avoid Fridays 4 PM+:** competes with end-of-week noise

**Followup sequence (if no response):**
1. **Initial DM:** Week 1, Tuesday–Thursday
2. **Wait 5–7 days**
3. **Followup comment:** React/comment on one of their recent posts with a thoughtful, non-promotional observation
4. **Wait 3 days**
5. **Second DM:** Light follow-up ("Wasn't sure if that landed. Worth a conversation?")
6. **Stop after second DM:** They've made their choice

**Response rate targets:**
- Initial message: 15–20% open rate expected
- DM responses: 8–10% reply rate from opens
- Conversion to call: 40–50% of responders will take a call

---

## Key Rules (All Templates)

1. **No pitch in the DM.** The pitch is in the 30-minute call, not the first message.
2. **Specific to them.** Mention something from their profile, a post they made, or a sector detail.
3. **Short.** No DM longer than 100 words (excluding signature).
4. **Value first.** "I might save you time/money" before "Let's talk."
5. **One CTA only.** Either "Call?" or "Worth talking?" — not both.
6. **Sign with your name.** Personal connection, not corporate.

---

## Personalisation Notes

**For sector-specific outreach:**
- Mention their specific sector (decorated goods, workwear, print, awards, etc.)
- Reference a problem unique to that sector (artwork approval, B2B ordering, production planning, etc.)
- Shows you know their business, not generic

**For company-specific outreach:**
- Mention something they've posted or shared recently
- Reference a challenge they've published about
- Shows you've done homework

**For referral/warm contact:**
- Always name the mutual contact
- Keep tone lighter, more casual
- Still include the credibility hook (audit, guarantee, experience)

---

## Sample Outreach Sequence (5-Day Window)

**Day 1 (Tuesday, 9 AM):** Send DM from Template 1 or 3 (credibility + offer)

**Day 5 (Friday, 10 AM):** If no response, comment on their most recent post with a thoughtful, non-promotional observation

**Day 8 (Monday, 10 AM):** Send short follow-up DM ("Wasn't sure if that landed. Still think we could have a valuable conversation?")

**Done after Day 8:** If no response after second DM, move on. They're not the right contact right now.

---

## Expected Outcomes (Per 20 DMs Sent)

- Approx. 3–4 responses
- Approx. 1–2 booked discovery calls
- Approx. 0–1 actual audits

**Quality over volume:** 10 highly targeted DMs will outperform 50 generic ones.
