## VARIANT C: When AI Actually Works (and When It Doesn't)

AI works in one scenario: when you understand the bottleneck, the process is documented, and the system is consistent enough to automate.

AI doesn't work in: chaos, undocumented processes, systems that don't talk to each other, or when you're hoping it'll fix a problem you haven't actually identified.

Example. Decorated goods business. Manual artwork approval is taking 8 hours a week.

Approach 1 (wrong): Buy an AI design checker. It arrives. Nobody knows how to use it. It doesn't integrate with the order system. It gets abandoned week three.

Approach 2 (right): Understand the approval process. Document it. Check what's actually slowing it down (turns out it's not checking the design, it's tracking who approved it and for what customer). Fix the tracking system. Then see if AI helps with the actual design checking.

Different answer. Same AI.
