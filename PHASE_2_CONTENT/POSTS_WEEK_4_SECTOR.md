## VARIANT A: The Artwork Approval Bottleneck

Every decorated goods and workwear business has the same bottleneck that nobody talks about: artwork approval.

An order comes in. Customer supplies artwork (or you do). Someone checks it. Is the file format right? Are the colours safe? Does it meet brand guidelines? Will it print?

That person has to say yes or no, track feedback, and send it back to the customer. And on a day with 15 orders, that's 15 rounds of communication.

Most businesses do this in email. Or WhatsApp. Or a spreadsheet.

Some software claims to handle it. But it doesn't integrate with orders, so people ignore it and go back to email.

That bottleneck costs money in three ways:

1. **Time.** Someone is checking every artwork, every revision, manually.
2. **Delays.** Because it's manual, orders wait. Artwork gets approved Tuesday when it should have been Monday.
3. **Errors.** A file gets missed, or the wrong file gets approved, and you only find out when it's on the print floor.

The fix is usually simple. Not software — workflow. Who needs to approve? What are they actually checking for? How does that trigger the next step?

Map that clearly, and everything else falls into place.

## VARIANT C: Order Management to Despatch — The Hidden Opportunities

From order to despatch, every decorated goods business loses time at the same three points:

1. **Artwork approval to production.** Order sits waiting for artwork sign-off. Production doesn't start until it's approved. Approval is manual. Slack time: 1–3 days.

2. **Production planning.** What's printing today? What's embroidering? What's being assembled? That answer comes from a combination of order system, spreadsheet, and someone in their head. Planning is reactive, not proactive. Slack time: 1–2 days, plus rework when priorities change.

3. **Picking and despatch.** Orders leave production. Are they picked correctly? Is quality checked? Or is picking manual, and mistakes get caught at the customer?

Here's what you get if you map and improve those three points:

- 48-hour order turnaround instead of 5–7 days
- 15% fewer picking errors
- Production can actually plan instead of react
- Customers get delivery dates they can count on
- Nobody's stressed at despatch
