## VARIANT 1: "The Three Things Breaking Your Order Management"

**Headline:**
What I See Regularly in Decorated Goods: Three Operational Bottlenecks

**Body:**

Working with workwear and decorated goods businesses, I see three things over and over:

**1. Artwork approval is where everything gets stuck.**

Someone designs a print. It goes to the customer for approval. The customer doesn't reply for two days. Meanwhile, production is ready. Then the customer approves a version with changes. Now the file is outdated. Now someone has to redo it. Now you're late.

The system doesn't manage this. It just creates folders and hope.

Real cost: 5–10 days per order. 20% of your capacity wasted on rework and delay.

**2. Order management is split across four different places.**

Customer orders on your portal. You track production in your workshop system. Artwork lives in Dropbox. Invoicing is in accounting software. Customer communications are in email.

Nobody knows what's actually happening. Your customer service team spends 2 hours a day on "where is my order" conversations because they have to check four places to answer.

Real cost: 15–20 hours a week in wasted time. One full FTE doing detective work instead of selling.

**3. Your B2B portal doesn't do what your customers need.**

You have a portal. It lets people order. But it doesn't:
- Show them real-time production status
- Let them reorder from previous templates without starting over
- Manage artwork approvals in one place
- Do bulk ordering easily
- Give them visibility into what's live vs. what's in draft

So they call instead. Your phone rings constantly. Your sales team spends half their time on order status calls instead of selling.

Real cost: 25–30% of your sales team's time, every week.

**Here's what I see when we fix these:**

Artwork approval goes from 5–10 days to 1–2 days. Production starts on time.

Your team stops doing detective work. They sell instead.

Your customers stop calling. They get answers from the portal.

Order cycle time drops 20–30%. Capacity improves. Revenue per team member improves.

## VARIANT 2: "Why Your B2B Portal Isn't Solving the Real Problem"

**Headline:**
The B2B Portal Bottleneck (And It's Not What You Think)

**Body:**

I work with a lot of workwear and decorated goods businesses. And almost all of them have something in common:

A B2B portal that isn't actually solving their biggest problem.

The portal exists. Customers can order on it. But here's what's actually happening:

**Customers still call because the portal doesn't do what they need.**

They can't see where their order is in production. They can't tell which designs they've used before. Artwork approval doesn't happen in the portal—it happens via email attachments. Bulk orders take forever. Templates for repeat customers don't exist.

So they pick up the phone.

Your sales team, which should be selling, is instead answering "Where is my order?" and "Can we remake this design?" every single day.

**Meanwhile, three other problems are growing:**

Artwork approval is choking your production schedule. Someone designs something. It gets emailed for approval. Customer doesn't respond for two days. Then they approve a version with changes. Designer has to redo it. Production is now late.

Order management is split across four systems. Customer order is in the portal. Production is in your workshop system. Artwork is in Dropbox. Invoicing is separate. Nobody knows what's actually happening, so someone has to spend time reconciling everything.

Your team is buried in manual work, not strategic work.

**Here's what actually needs to happen:**

The portal needs to do three things:

1. **Real-time visibility** — Customers see where their order actually is (submitted → approved → in production → quality check → shipped). They don't have to call.

2. **Artwork approval in one place** — Designs are uploaded, approved, rejected, or redlined in the portal. No email chains. Faster decision-making. Production starts on time.

3. **Reorder from templates** — Customers can order the same design again without starting from scratch. Faster. Fewer questions. More repeat revenue.

When that exists, the dynamic shifts:

Your customers stop calling because they have visibility.

Your sales team stops doing service recovery and starts actually selling.

Your production schedule doesn't slip because artwork is approved in 24 hours, not 5 days.

## VARIANT 3: "The Hidden Cost of Manual Artwork Approvals"

**Headline:**
What Workwear and Decorated Goods Businesses Don't See: The Artwork Bottleneck

**Body:**

I keep seeing the same thing in decorated goods and workwear operations:

Artwork approval is completely manual, and it's costing you 20–30% of your production capacity.

Here's the pattern:

Customer wants a custom design. Designer creates it. Gets sent to customer (usually via email attachment). Customer doesn't respond for 2–5 days. Designer follows up. Then customer approves with changes. Designer reworks it. More back-and-forth. Finally approved. Production is now a week behind.

Multiply that by 10–15 orders a week, and you've lost weeks of production time.

**But that's only part of the cost:**

Your designer is context-switching constantly, waiting for approvals. Your production team is idle because the bottleneck is earlier in the process. Your sales team is managing angry customers wondering why orders are late.

The workflow is broken.

**Here's what I see change when we fix it:**

Artwork approval moves from email to a managed process (usually in your portal or a shared system). Customer sees the design. Approves or requests changes *in one place*. Designer gets pinged in real-time, not three days later.

Approval time drops from 5–10 days to 1–2 days.

That's not a small thing. That's:
- Production starting on schedule
- Your designer's time spent on design, not email management
- Your team not burning time managing angry customers
- 20–30% more orders through the same people

**Real example:** A workwear company I worked with had artwork approval taking 6 days on average. When we moved that to a proper workflow, it dropped to 24 hours. They were able to take on 3 more customer projects that month with the same team.
