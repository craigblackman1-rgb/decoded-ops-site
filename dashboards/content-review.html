The Artwork Approval Bottleneck
Universal decorated goods pain
A
Every decorated goods and workwear business has the same bottleneck that nobody talks about: artwork approval. An order comes in. Customer supplies artwork (or you do). Someone checks it. Is the file format right? Are the colours safe? Does it meet brand guidelines? Will it print? That person has to say yes or no, track feedback, and send it back to the customer. And on a day with 15 orders, that's 15 rounds of communication. Most businesses do this in email. Or WhatsApp. Or a spreadsheet. Some software claims to handle it. But it doesn't integrate with orders, so people ignore it and go back to email. That bottleneck costs money in three ways: 1. Time. Someone is checking every artwork, every revision, manually. 2. Delays. Because it's manual, orders wait. Artwork gets approved Tuesday when it should have been Monday. 3. Errors. A file gets missed, or the wrong file gets approved, and you only find out when it's on the print floor. The fix is usually simple. Not software — workflow. Who needs to approve? What are they actually checking for? How does that trigger the next step? Map that clearly, and everything else falls into place. I work with garment decoration, print, embroidery, workwear, promo merch, awards, signs. That bottleneck is universal. Free 60-minute discovery to understand what's actually costing you. Let's talk.
B2B Portals That Actually Work
Workwear / B2B ecommerce focus
B
Workwear and decorated goods businesses that sell B2B have a problem: existing ecommerce platforms don't handle workwear workflows. Customers need custom ranges per site. Credit terms. Multi-site orders. Bulk pricing. Maybe personalisation per person. Shopify doesn't do that. WooCommerce doesn't do that. Standard ecommerce is built for B2C. So businesses either: 1. Stay with manual ordering (email, spreadsheet). Cost: hours, errors, bottleneck. 2. Buy ecommerce software that doesn't fit the use case. Cost: implementation, workarounds, unused features. 3. Build custom. Cost: £20–50k, 6–12 months, then you own it forever. There's a middle ground. Platforms like Demystify Commerce or Brightpearl integration. Not a generic ecommerce checkout — a B2B ordering interface that understands workwear. Custom ranges per customer. Multi-site. Credit management. Personalization. Integrated to your backend. Cost: £5–15k, 8–12 weeks, and it actually works for the customer and your team. The problem: most businesses don't know this option exists. They're stuck between "no system" and "buy something generic and hope." That's what I audit for. Software fit. What your customers actually need. What your business can run. Free discovery to understand your workflow. Let's talk.
Order Management to Despatch
Systems thinking, operations focus
C
From order to despatch, every decorated goods business loses time at the same three points: 1. Artwork approval to production. Order sits waiting for artwork sign-off. Production doesn't start until it's approved. Approval is manual. Slack time: 1–3 days. 2. Production planning. What's printing today? What's embroidering? What's being assembled? That answer comes from a combination of order system, spreadsheet, and someone in their head. Planning is reactive, not proactive. Slack time: 1–2 days, plus rework when priorities change. 3. Picking and despatch. Orders leave production. Are they picked correctly? Is quality checked? Or is picking manual, and mistakes get caught at the customer? Here's what you get if you map and improve those three points: - 48-hour order turnaround instead of 5–7 days - 15% fewer picking errors - Production can actually plan instead of react - Customers get delivery dates they can count on - Nobody's stressed at despatch Most businesses don't optimise these. They're too busy operating the existing workflow. That's exactly what an audit finds. I work with decorated goods, print, embroidery, workwear, promo merch, awards, signs. Free discovery to walk through your order-to-despatch process. Let's talk.
